Sustainability Through the Ages

Scroll through and explore Intel's journey throughout the years, discovering how innovation and sustainability evolved together to shape a greener future in technology.

Intel Sustainability Timeline

Key milestones in Intel's environmental leadership, from groundbreaking processors to ambitious climate goals.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore founded Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of innovation and future sustainability leadership.

Intel quickly became a leader in semiconductor innovation, helping shape the future of personal computing and global technology.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel introduced the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, beginning the digital revolution.

The Intel 4004 revolutionized electronics by integrating computing power into a single chip for the first time.

1978

8086 Processor

The Intel 8086 established the x86 architecture that still powers modern computing today.

The x86 platform became the foundation for PCs, servers, and enterprise computing worldwide.

1985

386 Processor

Intel launched the 386 processor, introducing powerful 32-bit computing and improving system efficiency.

The 386 processor enabled advanced multitasking and helped accelerate software innovation during the PC boom.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel reached peak greenhouse gas emissions and began major investments in renewable energy and emissions reduction.

This marked a turning point toward greener manufacturing and long-term sustainability commitments.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launched its RISE strategy focused on responsible, inclusive, sustainable, and enabling business practices.

The RISE strategy aligned Intel's environmental, social, and governance goals across global operations.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel announced plans to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across global operations by 2040.

Intel committed to expanding renewable energy use, improving efficiency, and reducing supply-chain emissions.

2023

99% Renewable Electricity

Intel achieved 99% renewable electricity use worldwide, dramatically reducing operational emissions.

This achievement positioned Intel among the leaders in renewable energy adoption within the tech industry.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosted its first Sustainability Summit, bringing together leaders to advance greener semiconductor manufacturing.

The summit encouraged collaboration between industry leaders, researchers, and policymakers to drive sustainable innovation.
