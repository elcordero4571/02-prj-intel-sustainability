Intel Sustainability Timeline

Key milestones in Intel's environmental leadership, from groundbreaking processors to ambitious climate goals.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore founded Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of innovation and future sustainability leadership.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel introduced the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, beginning the digital revolution.

1978

8086 Processor

The Intel 8086 established the x86 architecture that still powers modern computing today.

1985

386 Processor

Intel launched the 386 processor, introducing powerful 32-bit computing and improving system efficiency.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel reached peak greenhouse gas emissions and began major investments in renewable energy and emissions reduction.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launched its RISE strategy focused on responsible, inclusive, sustainable, and enabling business practices.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel announced plans to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across global operations by 2040.

2023

99% Renewable Electricity

Intel achieved 99% renewable electricity use worldwide, dramatically reducing operational emissions.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosted its first Sustainability Summit, bringing together leaders to advance greener semiconductor manufacturing.
